— A letter / Sylvain Beaumont, blender

The slowest part is waiting for what's already true.

My father bought the cellar in 1962 because the cliff behind it held still at fourteen degrees year-round. Twelve years later he opened the first amphora. The bergamot had become something my mother could not name; only smell, and decide it had been worth the wait.

We blend nothing in a hurry. Each year a single batch — fifty-four bottles, never more. They ship in the season the harvest demanded. If a vintage doesn't meet the cup, we hold it another year. We have not released a bad bottle in twenty-eight years because we have not released a bottle we doubted.

Four chapters, each measured in years not weeks.

  1. Source

    Six farms across Provence, Calabria, Sri Lanka. Handpicked at the morning the farmer says ready — never the morning the calendar does.

    Year 0 · harvest

  2. Age

    Sealed in clay amphorae set against the limestone cliff. Fourteen degrees, always. We open the seal once a year, smell it, write it down, close it again.

    Year 1 — 12 · cellar

  3. Blend

    By hand. By Sylvain. Each batch in a single afternoon — no more than fifty-four bottles, sometimes thirty-six. The number is what the year gave us.

    Final year · atelier

  4. Reserve

    Bottled by hand, sealed, signed. Each carries a chapter number out of the year's total. Yours is shipped within the season — by the farmer's calendar, not ours.
